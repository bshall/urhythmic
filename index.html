Rhythm Modeling for Voice Conversion

Authors: Benjamin van Niekerk, Marc-Andr√© Carbonneau, Herman Kamper

Abstract: Voice conversion transform source speech into the voice of a different speaker. While rhythm is an important factor for the perception of identity, it is not modeled in typical voice conversion systems. This paper introduce Urhythmic, an unsupervised method for rhythm conversion that does not require parallel data or text transcriptions. Using self-supervised representations, we segment source audio into units roughly corresponding to sonorants, obstruents, and silences. Then we model rhythm by estimating speaking rate or the duration distribution of each segment type. Finally, we match the target rhythm by time-stretching speech segments before synthesizing. Experiments show that Urhythmic outperforms existing unsupervised methods in terms of quality and prosody.
